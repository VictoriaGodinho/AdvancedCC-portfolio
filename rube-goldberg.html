Rube Goldberg Machine

Description

For this project, we had to create a Rube Goldberg machine, so I decided to base mine on a dream I had as a kid—an imaginative world where different "environments" existed, each offering a unique place to live. In my dream, everything was vibrant, filled with vivid colors, playful interactions, and whimsical landscapes that felt like stepping into a fantasy. I wanted to bring this vision to life by designing a machine where a marble travels through four distinct worlds, each representing a different environment.

The journey begins in Candyland World, where everything is pink, sugary, and sweet. Inspired by childhood fantasies of a place made entirely of candy, this world is filled with pink soft pastel hues, giving the impression of a dreamlike confectionary paradise. The marble then transitions into Fall World, where deep oranges create a cozy autumn atmosphere, evoking the crisp air, falling leaves, and the warmth of a seasonal change. Next, the marble enters Summer World, where bright yellows and blues dominate the scene, mirroring the vibrancy of sunny days, ocean waves, and the carefree energy of the season. Finally, the marble reaches Winter World, a landscape of whites, where the atmosphere is reminiscent of snow-covered wonderlands.

Each world’s color palette reflects the climate and essence of the environment, making the marble’s journey not just a physical movement through obstacles but also a visual and thematic transformation. The machine seamlessly blends nostalgia, playfulness, and dynamic interactions, turning a simple chain reaction into a storytelling experience.

Observations

Initially, my Rube Goldberg machine was meant to portray a completely different story—one based on my childhood memories of going to school. I had planned out a sequence that reflected the daily journey, incorporating elements that captured the feeling of nostalgia. However, after nearly completing the project, I lost all my progress twice, which was incredibly frustrating. Having to start over after so much effort was discouraging, and I struggled with the idea of rebuilding everything from scratch.

Instead of trying to recreate the same concept, I decided to take a different approach. I shifted my focus to a childhood dream I had of an imaginative world, turning the project into something more vibrant and playful. This change allowed me to experiment with color, storytelling, and world-building in ways I hadn’t originally planned.

Despite the setbacks, I gained a lot of knowledge throughout the process! Both technically and creatively. In the end, I feel much more satisfied with the result than I did with my original idea. What started as a frustrating loss turned into an opportunity to create something that truly resonates with me, making the final outcome even more rewarding.
