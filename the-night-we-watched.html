“The Night We Watched”

Final Video

Immersive Experience Video

Description

A projection and sound installation that shows a nighttime cityscape through apartment windows, each revealing everyday moments from different lives. Highlighting the quiet, shared experiences that connect us all.

Observations

My work explores the quiet intimacy of city life that often goes unnoticed. In New York City, we are a city of strangers but we are constantly surrounded by endless emotions and moments in time that each have their own story. Through projections and sound, I aim to recreate a late night feeling of walking around and looking at the endless windows and wondering what stories are taking place behind them.

My installation invites viewers to pause and reflect on these simple human moments; someone washing dishes, a party going crazy, someone falling asleep to the TV. These scenes are made to feel real and familiar and to show how connected we all are. With the projection, the soundscape will blend together city ambiance, individual sounds from the windows and soft jazz to create a gentle yet intriguing immersive atmosphere.

Progress

For this project I divided the work into two parts. First I collected the videos for the apartments (by both taking them myself and finding them on Artlist) and worked on the world building and animation. Finally, I made the final editing of the video, making an introduction and an ending to the animation made.
